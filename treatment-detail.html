What is Treatment?

Types of Treatment

  • Total Knee Replacement (TKR): Entire knee joint is replaced.
  • Partial Knee Replacement (PKR): Only damaged part is replaced.
  • Bilateral Knee Replacement: Both knees replaced together.
  • Revision Knee Replacement: Replacement of worn implant.

Who Should Get Treatment?

1. Severe Pain and Limited Mobility
  • Pain while walking, climbing stairs, or standing
  • Pain even during rest
2. Arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Post-traumatic arthritis
3. Chronic Swelling
  • Inflammation not improving with rest or medicines
4. Knee Deformity
  • Bowed legs or knock knees
5. Failure of Non-Surgical Treatments
  • Physiotherapy
  • Medications
  • Injections

Factors Affecting Cost

  • Implant Type: Standard vs premium implants.
  • Surgeon’s Expertise: Experienced surgeons may charge more.
  • Hospital Facilities: Advanced hospitals cost more.
  • City Location: Metro cities are usually more expensive.

Additional Pre- and Post-Surgery Costs

Pre-Treatment
  • Consultation: ₹1,000 – ₹2,500
  • Blood tests: ₹1,500 – ₹4,000
  • X-ray: ₹800 – ₹2,000
  • MRI: ₹5,000 – ₹12,000
Post-Treatment
  • Medicines: ₹2,000 – ₹5,000/month
  • Follow-ups: ₹1,000 – ₹3,000/visit
  • Physiotherapy: ₹10,000 – ₹50,000

Tips to Reduce Surgery Expenses

  • Choose all-inclusive hospital packages
  • Consider treatment in Tier-2 cities
  • Select cost-effective implants
